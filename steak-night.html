🛒 Ingredients — serves 2, scale up for the family

  • 2 steaks — scotch fillet or porterhouse, at least 2.5–3 cm thick, out of the fridge 20–30 min before cooking
  • A little neutral oil (for the pan) and olive oil (for rubbing the steaks)
  • Flaky salt and cracked black pepper — season generously, both sides and the edges
  • 30–40 g butter, 2–3 garlic cloves (smashed, skin on), a few sprigs thyme or rosemary — for the baste
  • Sides: 500 g–1 kg small potatoes, 4–6 truss or field tomatoes, a bunch of green beans or broccolini, 200–250 g cup mushrooms

🥕 From your box & our makers

  • Mushrooms - Cups — our #3 veg by volume, and a classic steak partner: sliced and pan-fried in the resting butter (Step 5)
  • Potatoes - Chats (small) - 5kg bags — halved and roasted alongside
  • Truss Tomatoes or Tomatoes - Field — roasted on the same tray
  • Beans - Green or Broccolini (bunch) — quick-steamed while the butter's foaming
  • Garlic - Imported (or Peeled Garlic 1kg) — for the baste and the mushrooms
  • Our beef — Beef - Scotch Fillet 2-pack, or the T-bone Steak 2-pack (the porterhouse portion, on the bone) — grass-fed & finished on the Mornington Peninsula, sold as monthly pre-order packs

🧰 Equipment · ⏱ Timing

  • A heavy pan (cast iron is ideal), tongs, a spoon for basting, a roasting tray, a meat thermometer if you have one
  • Start the oven first — the veg sides take 35–40 minutes; the steak itself is only about 15 minutes hands-on including the rest
  • Serves 2 as written — the beef packs come in 2s, so grab a second pack (or ask your butcher for more) for a bigger table
