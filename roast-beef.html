🛒 Ingredients — gravy ✓ from Andy's written recipe; the rest as stated in the video

  • A roasting joint of beef — topside (leanest, most economical), sirloin, or a rib roast (Andy's pick today). He gives no weight: “at the end of the day it's all about temperature”
  • Neutral high-temperature oil — grapeseed, avocado, or peanut (“if no one's got any allergies”)
  • Good-quality rock salt · black pepper (added after cooking, never before — Step 4 explains)
  • Roasting potatoes — a floury type (Andy uses Royal Blues; no weight given)
  • Beef tallow for the potatoes — supermarkets and butchers carry it; Andy rates it above duck fat
  • The gravy (verbatim from the written recipe): 2 carrots · 2 onions · 3 celery sticks · 4 rosemary sprigs · 200 ml red wine · 700 ml beef stock · 2 heaped tbsp flour · salt and pepper
  • Garnishes are your call, says Andy: Yorkshire puddings, cauliflower cheese, honey-glazed carrots, watercress, horseradish cream, bread sauce…

🧰 Equipment · ⏱ Timing

  • A heavy oven-proof pan or metal roasting tray (it goes on the stove later for the gravy), a pot and colander for the spuds, a whisk, a sieve, a board and a sharp knife
  • A meat thermometer is the tool Andy really wants you to own — pull the beef at 55°C core for medium
  • Oven 180°C fan-forced (Andy quotes ~190°C for a non-fan oven) · beef about 40 minutes, then trust the probe, not the clock · potatoes: ~25 min boil, then into hot fat with a flip at 10 · the beef rests while the potatoes finish and the gravy comes together — about 1½ hours all-in (optional day-before dry-brine)
  • Serves: Andy doesn't say — our estimate: the Silverside 1.5kg does a family Sunday table, with cold-roast-beef sandwiches on the Lievito tomorrow as the leftover win
