🛒 Ingredients — ✓ from Jack's written recipe (feeds 6–8)

  • Mash: 1 kg potatoes, peeled & halved — Jack lists Kestrel, Russet or Yukon Gold; our spud note is at Step 2
  • Mash: 100 g unsalted butter (softened) + 150 ml full-fat milk (warmed) + 2 free-range egg yolks; 4 Tbsp (30 g) parmesan for the top — on camera Jack skips the cheese
  • Filling: 2 Tbsp olive oil + 1 kg beef mince (90% lean in the video)
  • Filling: 1 carrot, 1 brown onion and 1 large celery rib — all grated — plus 3 cloves garlic, grated (Jack grabs 4 on camera)
  • Filling: 3 g flat-leaf parsley + the leaves of 3 thyme sprigs, finely chopped; 3 g rosemary optional
  • Filling: 2 Tbsp (50 g) concentrated tomato paste + 2 Tbsp (30 ml) Worcestershire sauce
  • Filling: 1 cup (250 ml) red wine (or an extra cup of stock — Jack's alcohol-free swap) + 1 cup (250 ml) vegetable or chicken stock + 1 cup frozen peas + seasoning to taste
  • Gluten-free? There's no flour in this recipe — pick a GF-labelled Worcestershire and stock and the whole pie is gluten-free (just skip the sourdough side)

🧰 Equipment · ⏱ Timing

  • A big pot (potatoes), a small saucepan (milk & butter), a large high-rimmed pan or pot (filling), a box grater + fine grater, a potato ricer or masher, and a roughly 26–30 cm baking dish
  • Oven 180 °C. About 40 minutes of relaxed hands-on (the potatoes boil while the filling builds), a 20–25 minute bake, then a 10–15 minute rest — about 1¼ hours end to end
  • Feeds 6–8 — the family freeze-half win: bake it in two smaller dishes and freeze one, or freeze cooled leftover portions; it reheats brilliantly on a school night
