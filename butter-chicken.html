🛒 Ingredients — ✓ from Andy's written list (serves 4 on the 2-thighs-each maths)

  • 8 chicken thighs (2 each), cut into strips — breast works well too
  • Marinade spices: 1 tsp ground turmeric · 1½ tsp ground cumin · 1½ tsp ground coriander · 1 tsp garam masala · 1 tsp chilli powder · 1 tsp kosher salt
  • Juice of 1 lemon · 120 ml Greek yoghurt
  • One garlic-ginger paste, split between marinade and gravy: 1 thumb-size piece of fresh ginger + 6 cloves garlic
  • Gravy fats & whole spices: 6 tbsp unsalted butter (used in halves) · 2 tbsp olive oil · 4 cardamom pods · 2 dried bay leaves · 4 whole cloves (the video adds cinnamon sticks too — see Step 7)
  • Gravy ground spices: ½ tsp turmeric · 1½ tsp cumin · 1 tbsp garam masala · 1 tsp chilli powder · 1½ tsp ground coriander
  • 1 large brown onion · 1 green chilli (optional, deseeded)
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes — or 6 fresh ripe tomatoes (see Step 10) · 60 g cashews
  • 240 ml (1 cup) water · 1–2 tbsp sugar (taste before it all goes in) · kosher salt to taste · 100 ml thickened cream
  • Coriander for garnish · steamed basmati rice or naan to serve (from the video)

🧰 Equipment · ⏱ Timing

  • Big bowl, microplane or fine grater, a heavy-based pot, an oven tray + your grill (broiler), any blender — and a fine sieve (non-negotiable, see Step 14)
  • Marinate 3 hours minimum (overnight is better) · about 40 minutes hands-on · sauce simmer 20–45 minutes (Andy went the full 45)
  • Serves 4 (2 thighs each) — even better the next day, and the sieved sauce base (end of Step 14) freezes brilliantly, so bank a batch
  • Naturally gluten-free served on rice (skip the naan and check your spice blends) · contains dairy and cashews
